Vesper

Description

Vesper Martini. The actual James Bond martini. Contrary to popular belief, the James Bond martini is not a "vodka martini, shaken not stirred". As it was written by Ian Fleming, the martini was called the Vesper and it likens itself to "drinking like a cloud" (as my brother stated after he drank it for the first time).

The original recipe calls for a Russian grain vodka of which I usually choose Belvedere. It also calls for Kina Lillet which actually ceased it's production in 1986 whick makes aquiring a bottle next to impossible. A good substitute which we will use is Lillet Blanc which can be found at any liquor store. Lastly, it calls specifically for Gordon's Gin which is a deliciously smooth and affordable gin that is very easy to find.

Without further ado. Let's drink!

Ingredients

  • 2oz Gordon's Gin
  • 1oz Belvedere Vodka
  • 1/2oz Lillet Blanc
  • 1 Large, thin Lemon Peel

Steps

  1. Put your martini glasses in the freezer
  2. In a shaker, combine the Gin, Vodka, and Lillet and shake until frosty
  3. Strain into your chilled glasses
  4. Roll and squeeze the lemon peel and then rim the glass with it
  5. Garnish with Lemon Peel
  6. Enjoy!
